Know Your Rights. Own Your Education.

If you have a disability — ADHD, anxiety, dyslexia, a physical condition, or anything else — the law says your school must give you what you need to learn. Most students don't know this. You do now.

Learn your rights

What is this site?

The guide your school counselor should have given you

If you have a disability and you're struggling in school, you are not alone — and you are not out of options. Federal law gives you the right to get support. But the system is confusing, the paperwork is overwhelming, and most students have no idea where to start.

I built this site because I've been there. I have ADHD, anxiety, and a fine motor delay. I spent years figuring out what accommodations I was entitled to, how to ask for them, and what to do when my school didn't follow through. I wanted to make it easier for the next student who feels stuck.

Everything here is based on real federal law — primarily IDEA, Section 504, and the ADA. I'll explain what that means in plain English. No legal jargon. No runaround. Just what you actually need to know.

Why I built this

The system shouldn't be this hard to navigate

I'm a high school junior with ADHD, anxiety, and a fine motor delay. For a long time, I didn't know what I was entitled to. I sat in meetings I didn't understand, signed documents I hadn't read, and accepted less than what the law said I deserved.

When I finally learned what my rights were, things changed. I started asking better questions. I pushed back when my accommodations weren't followed. I understood what was happening in my IEP meetings.

That knowledge shouldn't require hours of research to find. It should be one clear website, written by someone who's actually been through it. That's what this is.

1 in 5

Students in the U.S. have a learning or attention issue that affects how they learn in school.

7.5M+

Students received services under IDEA in the 2022–23 school year — but millions more qualify and never ask.

3 laws

IDEA, Section 504, and the ADA all protect students with disabilities. Most students have heard of none of them.

Not sure where to begin?

If you're new here, start with your rights. Then figure out whether a 504 or IEP fits your situation. Everything else follows from there.
